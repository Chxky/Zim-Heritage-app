# ZimHeritage — Government Alignment & Mandate Compliance

## 1. Heritage-Based Curriculum Framework (2024-2030)

The Zimbabwe Ministry of Primary and Secondary Education introduced the Heritage-Based Curriculum to replace the previous competency-based curriculum. ZimHeritage is fully aligned with this framework.

### Key Alignment Points:

| Curriculum Pillar | ZimHeritage Implementation |
|-------------------|----------------------------|
| **Heritage-based approach** | All subjects incorporate Zimbabwean history, culture, and heritage content |
| **Competency-based learning** | Lessons focus on understanding and application, not memorisation |
| **5 cross-cutting themes** | ICT, gender, inclusivity, environment, and life skills integrated across subjects |
| **Continuous assessment** | Built-in homework, quizzes, and progress tracking system |
| **Foundation learning (ECD)** | Visual aids and age-appropriate content for ECD A & B |

## 2. National Development Strategy 1 (NDS1) — Education Pillar

ZimHeritage directly contributes to NDS1 education objectives:

- **Objective 1**: Improve access to quality education → Free mobile access to curriculum content
- **Objective 2**: Enhance ICT in education → Digital-first learning platform
- **Objective 3**: Improve learning outcomes → AI-personalised tutoring & instant feedback
- **Objective 4**: Strengthen teacher capacity → Digital tools for lesson planning & assessment

- Enabling lifelong learning through accessible educational resources
